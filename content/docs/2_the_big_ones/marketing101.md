Your goal as a copywriter is simple:

To persuade your readers to *take action* after reading your campaign.

Achieving this goal takes no special talent, and you don't need to be a creative genius.

You just need to understand two things:

* Who you're writing to, and
* What you're selling

The Research Questions outlined here in the Persuasion Playbook are aimed at helping you uncover those two fundamentals.

Before diving into the research side of your project, though, it's useful to go over these two pillars of your copy.

That way, you know what to look out for and how to use it when you sit down to write your campaign.

Let's break it down.

### The Nature of Your Product

Your product---be it a physical item, service, or concept---satisfies two sets of needs.

* The solution to a practical problem
* Offering a unique emotional appeal

While many tend to think of the practical side of a product---a set of wheels to get around town, a computer to do your work---it's the *emotional appeal* that persuades your readers to take action.

To give you an example, there's a local ice cream shop a block from my house. My kids and partner and I sometimes walk there after school for a frozen treat.

Now, the thing with ice cream is that it's food. But I'm certainly not thinking about nourishing my children's growing bodies when I buy them a cone of frozen cream, sugar, and food dyes.

What's important to me is them being happy. More specifically, them being happy with *me*. You'd be crazy to sell ice cream as a way to help your kids grow up to be strong and healthy...

But its deliciousness? Its creamy goodness? Convincing the ones you love that life with you is a sweet treat?

That shit sells.

### The Nature of Your Reader

Similar to understanding the emotional appeal of your product, your research should be aimed at coming to understand the emotional needs of your reader.

In other words, it's not enough to know you're selling a [car] to a [middle-aged soccer mom]. You have to dig deeper.

* What makes her tick?
* What problems does she face on the daily?
* What are her responsibilities, fears, frustrations, aspirations, and dreams?
 Come to understand her emotional needs. How she wants to see herself. Then treat her accordingly.

This level of understanding is plain-old getting to know someone. Ask questions and pay attention.

The folks you talk to will practically write your copy for you.



### How to Use the Playbook

Now that you have a better idea of what you're looking for...

It's *research*. And the more you do of it, the easier time you'll have coming up with creative solutions to your problems. Your magic headline, your persuasive pitch, it really comes down to knowing two things...


Once you have a handle on these fundamental pillars, your copy then links your reader---their goals and desires---and your product to create a *market*.

And the better you know your market, the better your copy.
